What does it take to survive in the desert?

Las Vegas is in the Mojave Desert. The average yearly rainfall in Las Vegas is only about 4.2 inches! Temperatures regularly reach over 100°F during the day in the summer and can be below freezing in the winter. Survival in the Mojave is no small feat! The animals that live and thrive in and around Las Vegas are adept at finding the three essential things for survival: water, food and shelter. Visit each page above to learn how different mammals, reptiles and birds survive the harsh desert conditions.

Water

Water in the desert can be difficult to find, however the animals that live in the Mojave are highly skilled at searching it out! They are also experts at using it efficiently. Some are so efficient that they don't even have to drink water!

Food

Like water, finding food in the desert can be unpredictable at the best of times. The animals that live here have different strategies for finding and storing food to aid in their survival.

Shelter

Shelter is a necessity to protect from extreme desert temperatures and potential predators! Some animals build shelters of their own, some steal them from others and some make use of the desert landscapes to keep them safe.
